Search Tips

You can use these tips to help you find what you are looking for, and often more precisely:

  • You can type part of the word you're looking for, then an asterisk (*), to find anything starting with those letters, e.g., psycho*
  • To find a phrase (words in order), surround the phrase in quotes, e.g., "primal fear"
  • You can combine multiple terms, requiring both or either to be present (AND and OR respectively.), e.g., fear or loathing

See below for a summary of some of the key search operators. There's a more complete explanation on the help pages. Click here for more information.

Term Function How to Use Example
* (asterisk) A wildcard, matches one or more characters anywhere in the search term. Add the asterisk to the end of a partial word, or even in the middle psychoanal* matches psychoanalysis, psychoanalytic, psychoanalyst, …
"" (quotation marks) Finds an exact phrase Use quotation marks around the phrase “chronic hypochondria”
~ (tilde) at the start of the word Finds variations of a term using an industry thesaurus Place the ~ at the start of the word. ~trauma Finds all documents matching trauma and all synonyms as derived from an industry dictionary (Klumpner)
AND Finds matches with BOTH terms Separate the words with AND. You can also just use a space. polymer and blue
OR Finds one term or another term Separate terms with OR. hypochondria or feigning
NOT Finds one term but not another Separate terms with “not” House not Senate Hypochondria not chronic
"word1 word2"~10 Finds terms that are within a certain number of words of each other (e.g. 10) Put words in quotes and add a tilde and the number of words which can occur between the search terms "smoke transference"~5 "aggressive tobacco"~5 finds “smoke transference ” and “tobacco aggressive ” within 5 words of each other (in either order)
